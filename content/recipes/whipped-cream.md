## Why Make It?

Homemade whipped cream tastes fresher than the tub or can, and you can control the sweetness. It is quick enough for pies, hot chocolate, fruit, pancakes, and last-minute desserts.

## Ingredients

- 1 cup cold heavy whipping cream
- 1 to 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract, optional

## Instructions

1. Chill a mixing bowl and beaters if you have time.
2. Add cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla to the bowl.
3. Beat on medium-high speed until soft peaks form.
4. Stop before it turns grainy or starts becoming butter.

## Storage

Refrigerate covered for up to 24 hours. Re-whisk briefly before serving if it softens.

## Cost Comparison

Homemade whipped cream can cost more than shelf-stable topping but usually costs less than premium refrigerated whipped cream. The flavor and ingredient control are the main win.

## What's In The Box?

Packaged whipped toppings may include oils, corn syrup, stabilizers, gums, and artificial flavors. Homemade whipped cream uses cream, sweetener, and optional vanilla.
