## Why Make It?

Homemade butter is one of the fastest from-scratch swaps you can make. It turns one ingredient into fresh butter and real buttermilk with no special equipment beyond a jar.

## Ingredients

- 1 cup cold heavy cream
- Pinch of salt, optional

## Instructions

1. Pour the cream into a clean mason jar, filling it no more than halfway.
2. Seal the lid tightly and shake until the cream thickens, clumps, and separates into butter and buttermilk.
3. Pour off the buttermilk and save it for baking.
4. Rinse the butter under very cold water, pressing gently until the water runs mostly clear.
5. Mix in salt if using, then shape and chill.

## Storage

Store covered in the refrigerator for up to 1 week.

## Cost Comparison

Homemade butter is usually closest in cost to premium butter, but it gives you fresh buttermilk as a bonus. It is most useful when cream is on sale or you want control over salt and freshness.

## What's In The Box?

Store-bought butter can include cream, salt, natural flavors, or coloring depending on the brand. Homemade butter keeps it simple: cream, plus salt if you want it.
