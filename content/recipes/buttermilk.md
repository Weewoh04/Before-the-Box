## Why Make It?

Homemade buttermilk is a quick fix when a recipe calls for buttermilk and you do not want to buy a whole carton. It adds tenderness and tang to pancakes, biscuits, muffins, and dressings.

## Ingredients

- 1 cup milk
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice or white vinegar

## Instructions

1. Add lemon juice or vinegar to a liquid measuring cup.
2. Pour in milk until it reaches 1 cup total.
3. Stir and let it sit for 5 to 10 minutes.
4. Use once the milk looks slightly thickened or curdled.

## Storage

Use right away for best results, or refrigerate covered for up to 2 days.

## Cost Comparison

This shortcut is often cheaper than buying a full carton when you only need a cup. It also reduces waste if buttermilk is not something you use often.

## What's In The Box?

Carton buttermilk may include cultured milk, salt, stabilizers, or added gums depending on the brand. The homemade shortcut uses milk plus acid.
