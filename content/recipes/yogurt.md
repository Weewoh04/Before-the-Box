## Why Make It?

Homemade yogurt turns milk into a flexible staple for breakfasts, smoothies, sauces, baking, and snacks. It is a good way to skip extra sugar and choose exactly how thick or tangy you want it.

## Ingredients

- 4 cups milk
- 2 tablespoons plain yogurt with live active cultures

## Instructions

1. Warm the milk to 180 F, stirring often.
2. Cool the milk to about 110 F.
3. Whisk a little warm milk into the plain yogurt, then stir it back into the pot.
4. Cover and keep warm for 6 to 12 hours, until set.
5. Chill before serving.

## Storage

Store covered in the refrigerator for up to 1 week. Save a few tablespoons to culture your next batch.

## Cost Comparison

Homemade yogurt is often cheaper than buying individual cups, especially if you buy milk by the gallon. The savings grow if your household eats yogurt often.

## What's In The Box?

Store-bought yogurt may include milk, cultures, sugar, flavors, starches, gums, or preservatives depending on the type. Homemade yogurt needs milk and a live-culture starter.
